Our Curriculum

At Nyanchwa Adventist Preparatory School, we follow Kenya's Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) designed to develop well-rounded learners with knowledge, skills, and values.

Our curriculum emphasizes:

  • Critical thinking and problem-solving skills
  • Communication and collaboration abilities
  • Creativity and imagination
  • Digital literacy and technology integration
  • Character development and values education
  • Hands-on learning experiences

We go beyond textbooks to provide experiential learning that prepares children for real-world challenges while nurturing their God-given talents and abilities.

Our Teaching Approach

Modern, effective methods that engage and inspire learners

Learner-Centered

Students actively participate in their learning through hands-on activities, discussions, and collaborative projects.

Inquiry-Based

We encourage curiosity and critical thinking through exploration, questioning, and problem-solving activities.

Technology Integration

Modern teaching aids and digital tools enhance learning experiences and prepare students for the digital age.

Interactive Learning

Group work, presentations, and peer learning foster communication skills and collaborative abilities.

Differentiated Instruction

We tailor teaching methods to accommodate different learning styles and individual student needs.

Continuous Assessment

Regular formative assessments help track progress and provide timely feedback for improvement.

Assessment & Reporting

Evaluation System
  • Continuous Assessment Tests (CATs) - Weekly and bi-weekly assessments
  • Mid-Term Exams - Comprehensive evaluation at term midpoint
  • End-Term Exams - Final assessment covering all topics
  • Project Work - Hands-on assignments and presentations
  • Progress Reports - Detailed reports sent to parents each term
  • Parent-Teacher Meetings - Regular consultations on student progress
